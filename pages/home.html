Welcome to Celbridge

Nestled along the River Liffey in County Kildare, Celbridge is a town steeped in history, heritage, and community spirit. From its early settlement to its role in Ireland’s political and industrial past, Celbridge offers a unique glimpse into the story of Ireland itself.

  • Get Started

A Town of Landmarks

Celbridge is home to Castletown House, Ireland’s first Palladian mansion and one of the grandest country houses in the nation. The town’s streets and surrounding lands are dotted with historic churches, old mills, and stately homes that reflect centuries of change and growth.

  • Learn More

Life by the Liffey

The River Liffey has shaped Celbridge for generations, powering its once-thriving mills and offering a natural beauty that still draws walkers and visitors today. The river remains a thread that connects Celbridge’s industrial past with its vibrant present.

  • Learn More

William Conolly

Born in Donegal and rising to become Speaker of the Irish House of Commons, William Conolly built Castletown House and reshaped Celbridge in the early 18th century. His wealth and ambition left a legacy that defined the town’s modern identity.

  • Learn More

Arthur Guinness

Born in Celbridge in 1725, Arthur Guinness laid the foundations of Ireland’s most famous stout. From his first home on Main Street to signing the legendary 9,000-year lease at St. James’s Gate, his legacy blends local roots with global impact.
