Castletown House & Parklands

Ireland’s landmark Palladian house and the green heart of Celbridge — a place where architecture, river, and wildlife meet. Stroll the tree-lined avenues, trace the River Liffey, and step inside a house that shaped taste and society in the 18th century.

The Castletown Story

Open the milestones to skim the house’s journey from power-house to public treasure.

Early 1700s — Vision & purchase

William Conolly acquires estates at Celbridge and sets out to create a residence that reflects national ambition.

1722–1729 — Building the house

Castletown rises in a new Palladian idiom. The residence becomes a focal point for political, social, and cultural life.

18th–19th c. — Parklands shaped

Avenues, water features, and woodland walks take form; the Liffey corridor frames views and leisure.

20th–21st c. — Conservation & access

Restoration and stewardship open the house and grounds to the public, balancing heritage and habitat.

Tips for Your Visit

  • Wear comfortable shoes — paths range from gravel to grass.
  • Keep dogs on leads near grazing and wildlife areas.
  • Bring a camera: morning and golden hour are best for the Liffey views.
